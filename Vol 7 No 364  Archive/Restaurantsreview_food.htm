Food      

There is a great variety to choose from at JOI and this includes vegetarian dishes as well. The list goes on and customers will have a hard time selecting their pick as everything on the menu reads yummy.

 

The Chicken Masala as well as the Mutton that the restaurant offers are both quite tasty dishes. Spicy as well as rich in ingredients, both dishes are served in a brass holder positioned above a table-top food heater, to keep the sauces hot at all times. Although the mutton was really tender and tasted heavenly, it was a bit watery and did not have the particular texture expected.
 

Moreover, the side dish of the customers preferred rice, also served in a brass pot, looked a bit old and tasted a bit stale. It looked and tasted like it had been cooked the morning of and microwaved before serving. The accompanying naan of choice on the other hand, smelt, felt and tasted as fresh as it can get.

 

The thing about Indian food is that one will intend to recognize the mystery of the ingredients used in each dish. Each bite is an exploration of tastes that one will intend to identify. But whether one in fact can or not, it is guaranteed that they will enjoy what they order. Diners just have to be wise in ordering  their dishes to be prepared for mild, medium or spicy.
