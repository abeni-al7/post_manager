Business News In Brief
 

Factory

 

Investors Erecting Engine Oil Factory with over 670M Br
 

Foreign investors have started erecting an engine oil plant with an outlay of over 670 million Birr in East Harereghe Zone, head of the Zonal Works and Urban Development office said. The investors are going to produce the oil from castor seeds and have been granted 13.9ht of land for the construction of the factory and another 8,000ht for growing castor plant. The factory is expected to employ 1,500 nationals while the castor farms will create an additional 4,000 jobs. With a capital of 109 million Birr, 80 investors have so far been issued licenses to engage in industry, real estate development, agriculture, construction, floriculture and other investment activities in 12 woredas.

 

 

 

Source: The Daily Monitor, April 17, 2007
 

Packaging

 

Five Carton Factories Licensed with over 307M Br Begin Construction
 

Five of the seven factories licensed with over 307 million Birr capital to manufacture cartons in Oromia State have commenced construction works. Oromia Investment Commissioner, Alemu Sime, said that the construction of the factories has been launched in Legetafo, Sebeta and Kaki areas on 9.5ht. The remaining two are under preparation to launch the construction work, the commissioner said. The Construction of the factories would solve the problem of packaging materials being imported from overseas with high foreign currency, would provide cartons for agro-industry and industrial produces and solve the serious shortage of packaging of flowers.

 

 

Source: The Daily Monitor, April 17, 2007

Network

 

AAU Network Upgrade Complete
 

Huawei Technologies Co. Ltd. has finalised the installation of the new network of Addis Abeba University (AAU) as per the donation amounting to over one million dollars, using Huawei's own data communication products (such as switches and routers). Huawei's viewpoint video conferencing terminal is also deployed in this network and will allow for remote teaching using this technology, which was made easy by the new gigabit network. The official inauguration ceremony was held in AAU Senate Hall on April 13, 2007. Established in 1988, Huawei Technologies is a private high-tech enterprise which specialises in research and development, production and marketing of communications equipment, and provides customised network solutions for telecom carriers in mobile, fixed optical data and communications networks and value added services.

 

 

Source: Press Release, April 13, 2007

Refueling

 

Shell Ethiopia Brought State of Art Jet A1 Refueling Vehicles

 

Shell Ethiopia brought one brand new refueler while refurbishing another with state of the art refueling vehicles worth well over six million Birr to ensure and strengthen the efficiency of aviation fuel refueling activities at Bole International Airport. During the inauguration ceremony of the refueling vehicles held in April 12, 2007 at Bole International Airport, Mr. Edgar Omoto, general manager of Shell Ethiopia Ltd asserted that the new vehicles would double the refueling capacity of the company in terms of volume of fuel and efficiency to satisfy the Jet A1 demand for both national flag carriers as well as international airlines that fly to and from Addis Abeba.

 

Source: Press Release, April 13, 2007

Bank

 

CBE Opens Two New Branches
 

The Commercial Bank of Ethiopia (CBE) has opened its 185th branch in the Northern part of Addis Abeba on April 10, 2007. Opening the branch, Ato Abie Sano, president of CBE said that the newly inaugurated branch, called 'Sheger', is opened following thorough feasibility and profitability studies. The President recalled that CBE opened six additional branches in the city over the past three months alone. He also noted that the Bank's services are showing marked improvements following the implementation of the BPR (Business Process Reengineering) studies in various units of the institution. Meanwhile, CBE opened its 186th branch in Werabe town of Selte Woreda, Southern Nations Nationalities and Peoples’ State (SNNPS) on April 14, 2007. CBE has correspondent account relationships with 40 renowned foreign banks and maintains SWIFT bilateral arrangements with more than 540 foreign banks. CBE is a principal-plus member of Visa International. Hence it is investing in the required infrastructure to set up Visa-branded card payment system.

 

Source: Press Release, April 14, 2007

Aid

 

 

Nation Concludes 4.6B Br Multilateral Loan, Grant Accords

 

The government of Ethiopia has signed 4.6 billion Birr loan and grant agreements with multilateral funding sources during the first half of this budget year, the Ministry of Finance and Economic Development (MoFED) announced. The Ministry concluded the agreements with the European Union(EU), World Bank(WB), African Development Bank and UN agencies, according to a document obtained from the Ministry. Out of the total agreement, over 2.5 billion Birr was obtained in loans, it was learnt.

 

Source: The Ethiopian Herald, April 14, 2007

Harvest

 

 

Zones Reap 773,000Qts Harvest

 

About 773,000 at of harvest was reaped from the land developed through irrigation in Bale, Borena and Guji Zones of Oromia State, the State's Irrigation Development Authority said. Head of the South Branch Office with the Authority, Ejara Tola, said yesterday that the stated volume of harvest was collected from 14,000ht of land developed through irrigation over the past nine months. He said over 25,000 farmers took part in the irrigation development. The harvest reaped during the reported period exceeds that of same period last year by 200,000 qt, he said, adding the farmers are expected to collect over 300,000 qt of harvest in the next three months.

Source: Addis Zemen, April 16, 2007
