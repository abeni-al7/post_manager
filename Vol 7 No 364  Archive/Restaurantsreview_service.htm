Service    

The service at JOI is both friendly and formal. There are three waitresses and one head waiter serving customers, dressed in traditional Indian garbs of beige pants, same colored long shirt and a black vest with designs on it.

 

When seated, one of them approaches your table with a smile on their face and a menu in both hands. After giving you a small nod, they hand you the menu and ask for your drink order. Once the diners select their choice of beverage, they leave the table to give them a moment to peruse over and ponder on the list of food items and impressively long list of beverages of an alcoholic and non-alcoholic nature, recorded on an off white background in black print.
 

The menu is most probably one of the best that one will encounter in Addis Abeba. It is a black bound catalog of well-laminated pages, with small yet clear descriptions of food items and beverages.
 

Those customers that do not feel comfortable ordering or want a suggestion need not worry as those in waiting are quite capable of making great recommendations and describing the meals comfortably in both Amharic and English.
 

 The billing system is top of the line. It is an electronic print, with the restaurant’s name, address, contacts and the waiters on it. It also has a clear list of the items taken and the prices, including the taxes.
