Land

 

Ministry Undertakes Land Holding Management Support Programme

 

The Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Development has been undertaking the land holding and management support programme in four regional states. The Ministry has been undertaking the program in 24 woredas and 90 kebeles in collaboration with Oromia, Tigray, Amhara and Southern Nations Nationalities and Peoples' Regional States. The programme, which is being financed and technically assisted by USAID and ARD, would be implemented for a period of three years. The Ethiopian land holding and management support programme is aimed at modernising the process of land holding using technology and training.
 

 
Source: The Daily Monitor, March 15, 2007
 

Coffee

 

Coffee Enterprise Says Secures Over 78m Br in Revenue
 

Coffee Plantation and Development Enterprise said it has collected over 78 million birr from the supply of coffee for foreign and local markets over the past six months. Representative of the Enterprise's Manager Sisay Asrat said that the Enterprise collected the revenue from the supply of dry and washed coffee Arabica. The Enterprise has obtained over 66 million birr revenue from the export of washed coffee to Germany, Japan, Switzerland, England, France and Taiwan, among others. The number of Ethiopian Coffee buyer companies has increased to 17 from only three, he said adding that the Enterprise has started exporting its products for the first time to America. The enterprise has secured close to 13 million birr from the sale of unwashed coffee for local market over the reported period.

 

 
Source: The Daily Monitor, March 13, 2007

Ministry Close to Finalise Deals with Oil Companies
 

The Ministry of Mines and Energy carried out negotiations with four foreign companies for petroleum exploration and development in Ogaden, Kalub Hilala, Gambella and Benishangul Gumuz, a public relations official with the Ministry said. The Ministry has finalised negotiations and signed a petroleum exploration and development agreement with one foreign company, he added. The negotiations were made with Global Oil and Energy Resources, Petronas, K and S as well as Westminister Oil Limited companies.

 

 

 
Source: The Daily Monitor, March 13, 2007
