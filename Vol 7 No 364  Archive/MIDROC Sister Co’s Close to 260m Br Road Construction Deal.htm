MIDROC Sister Co’s Close to 260m Br Road Construction Deal

  
 

Involves “most difficult road in Ethiopia’s history”

  
     
 
 















 

  

Derba-MIDROC Cement Factory Model
Haile Assegdie, executive director of Derba-MIDROC
 


Derba-MIDROC Cement PLC has hired MIDROC Construction Ethiopia PLC for 260 million Br to undertake the construction of 42Km of road at the factory site in Derba, West Shoa Zone of the Oromia Regional State.
 

The contract was signed on April 19, 2007, at the Derba-MIDROC offices on Jomo Kenyata Avenue by Haile Assegdie, executive director of Derba-MIDROC, and Shimeles Eshete, deputy general manager of MIDROC Construction.

 
 

Derba-MIDROC was recently licensed by the Ministry of Trade and Industry (MoTI), while another 15 companies are registered, since the supply shortage two years ago. It was established with a capital of 2.4 billion Br, Sheik Mohammed Al-Amoudi owning eight per cent, and the rest owned by his company, MIDROC Ethiopia. The company was granted 123.42ht of land in Chancho woreda in the West Shoa Zone of the Oromia Regional State. China National Building Materials (CNBM) was contracted in January 2007 to construct a factory with a daily production capacity of 7,000tns, making it the largest factory in the country to date.
 

The company will also procure mining equipments as well as constructing six six-storey apartments for employees and 12 one-storey buildings for executive residences.

 

The road construction will involve three sections. The first involves upgrading a 20Km gravel road from Chancho to Derba to asphalt concrete. The second is an 8.2Km new asphalt road construction from Derba to the factory site.
 

The third section, which is also the more challenging, is a 13.8Km part-asphalt, part-gravel road, which will link the factory sight with the quarry circling a steep hill.
 

Haile Assegdie told Fortune, “When MIDROC construction has completed the construction of this road, it will be a historic achievement on the most difficult road in Ethiopia’s history.”
 

The straight-line distance from the factory site to the quarry is said to be just one kilometre, whereas the tortuous road will extend it to nearly 14Km.  The road will be constructed in such a way that it will enable the easy movement of trucks up and down the slope between the site and the quarry.
 

“I guess that this is going to be highly challenging for us,” said Shimeles.
 

MIDROC Construction has 18 months to complete the work.
 

Derba-MIDROC will float a tender for the construction of the employee and executive facilities in the near future, according to Haile. This part of the project includes, in addition to the residential quarters, a school, kindergarten, stadium, bank, post office, clinic, supermarket and cafeteria. The design of these buildings has been completed by Universal Consultants. The total real estate budget is 300 million Br.
 

Ries Engineering, MOENCO, and Equatorial Business Group, are also bidding to supply Caterpillar, Komatsu and Volvo mining equipments. Haile said that price negotiations are underway with these companies, whose highest offer was 180 million Br. He declined to comment further on the issue. The winning company will be announced by the end of this week, according to Haile.

Derba-MIDROC is expected to become fully operationally by March 2009, when it will have 500 employees.
