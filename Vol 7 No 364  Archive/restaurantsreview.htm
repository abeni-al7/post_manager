Cafe Review
Name :

Red Bean Café
Location: Off Africa Avenue, on the gravel road leading to MEDCO Biomedical Collage

 
Service      

Red Bean offers an all male wait staff clad in black trousers, a red polo t-shirt with the logo of the café stitched into it. The waiters also wore an ankle length black French apron and a black baseball cap, also with the logo of the café. The casual outfits that the young men were wearing were simple, neat and very appealing to the eye of the customer. There was also a manager  present on the occasion of all the reviews, wearing a pair of kakis and a black satin shirt; he was also garbed in the same baseball cap as the staff that he was diligently supervising.
 

It was a matter of walking into the café and you already had the attention of a waiter. Once seated, there was someone standing over you to ask what you would like. For people that were not familiar with the various items that were offered, a menu is supplied promptly, and the waiters are paying attention such that they are at the table as soon as the diners head is lifted from their reading.

But it does not end at that. After the customer has placed the order, and is enjoying whatever blend that they have chosen, there is very good follow up on both the part of the waiter and manger to make sure that all the items that were served satisfied the customer and that there was nothing more that could be added.
 

The menu that is offered is well thought out and very attractive, sleek and neat in its appearance. There are no spelling mistakes as is often the case with many establishments.
 

Added to the professional billing system used by the café, the service offered at the Red Bean is something that you would hope to find in any eatery or café in town.

 
 
Food    

The menu offers a selection of breakfast dishes and sandwiches giving the café goer plenty of choices in the instance of a quick lunch. On one review, the reviewer decided to choose the cheeseburger, a wonderful choice, which qvite appetizing. The cheeseburger, large enough to satisfy but not overwhelming, came with crisp and fresh French fries, the sort that is an illusion at most eateries that offer such dishes.

 

Another option that was sampled from the dishes was the club sandwich. Offering a creamy interior with all the traditional ingredients; the presentation of the sandwich alone was enough to satisfy the appetite.

 

The reviewer that sampled the chocolate cake had for a moment thought they had died and gone to chocolate heaven. With the perfect blend of sweet and bitter chocolates, thick rich icing, and soft moist cake, this was the perfect choice for any desert fan. Coupled with the Irish Cream Latté, the combination of the creamy drink with the texture of the cake made this experience special.
 

In addition to offering all of the regular coffees and macchiato that you would find at just about any café in town, the Red Bean has also developed its own blends of coffee and traditional Ethiopian grains to make concoctions such as the bulla and chocolate macchiatos.

 

It is almost difficult trying to choose from the different kinds of coffees that are available at the Red Bean, considering the variety in their choices. Fortune's reviewers give the café a standing ovation in this regard and would recommend it to anyone who is willing to listen.

 
 
Price      

The pricing at the Red Bean is a bit odd, with some items being affordable while others are a little on the steep side. The sandwiches range from 13-18 Br. Hot drinks go for three to eight Birr, with sodas being sold at 2.50-3.50 Br. Pastries are a bit on the expensive side, going for up to eight Birr. Interestingly, these prices do not include 15pc VAT and a five per cent service charge, that the establishment informs you of on the bottom of every page in the menu.
 

One has to consider that it is not just the food that the prices are for but also include the service and environment. Although the reviewers at Fortune were pleased with all aspects, prices on the food and drinks are not affordable for the average Ethiopian.

 

 
 
Environment    

Cute, charming, tasteful, well thought about, intimate, colourful, themed, and pretty are just the tip of the iceberg when choosing the right adjectives to describe the space that the Red Bean is situated in. The café sits on the ground floor of the building with its front wall framed by large windows. You are greeted with simple, large, yet attractive metal chairs and tables on the outside of the café, although as you are parking or walking up, you are also able to get a full view of the interior as well.

 

There are two entrances to the café located on either side of the glass wall. As you enter through the left door, there is a black leather couch set that is situated against the opposite wall. The space is filled with black furniture, the chairs are small and wooden with a modern cut to them. They are situated around a small black metal table. Although there is more than enough room to accommodate a number of customers, the space does not give the impression of being cramped.

 

To the right of the entrance, the service bar, with glass pastry displays and black coffee machines, is located in front of a chalk board that has the breakfast choices written on it. The service bar is manned by three or four people, all diligent behind their fortress of the ultra modern machines and cash register.

 

The walls are painted in shades of red, using a sponging system that added shades and textures. The lighting is suspended from black tracks, and the overall effect is soothing and smooth. The choice of photographs is also on par with the decór, with beautiful photos of fresh coffee hung on all walls in thick black frames.
 

Located less than 50m off Africa Avenue, transportation to this café, whether for pedestrians or for drivers is ideal.

 
 
Sanitation        

The male and female bathrooms that are offered are in the lobby of the ground floor of the building, although the reviewers were not sure if the bathroom was for that section of the building or for the café alone. On the occasion of one review, there was no water, and so the female bathrooms were locked.

 

The male facilities offered a toilet, in a separate stall, a urinal and a sink. The male bathroom offered soap, toilet paper and a mirror, although most of the toilet paper was on the floor and seat of the toilet rather than on the roll. The female bathrooms did not offer the urinal service, although the rest of the amenities were identical to that of the males. The space was not as clean as it should have been and offered water and dirt on the floors, which gave the reviewers the impression that more than just the customers of the café were making use of it.

 
 
Parking      

Although there is not a set parking lot to speak of, there is a concrete strip directly in front of the café that offers parking spaces for about 10-12 cars. But this does not mean that there is not ample parking, as the other side of the gravel road is also used. The café is able to cater to about 20 cars at a time that is with sharing the space with the tenants of the building that it is housed in. Being that it is off the main road, there is no fear of traffic congestion or having to wait some time for a parking space. There are two very professionally uniformed security guards that are present at all times, easing the concerns of drivers that park in the space.

 
 
 
 

Restaurant Review
Name :

Top Cuisine and cafe

  
Location:

Located on Haile Gebresellasie Avenue, on the sixth floor of the building next to the Waryt Building
 
 
Service        

The number of waiters varied between two and three on separate reviews, all had two females, while there was a third male that appeared to be a head waiter that was spotted on one lunch review.
 

The appearance of the waitresses was wanting in neatness, with their hair unkempt and their aprons slightly dirty on two separate reviews. They all wore black trousers with white shirts, on one review; the waitresses were wearing maroon French aprons over their trousers, but this trend was not maintained throughout all three reviews, with the waitresses not wearing anything on top of their trousers on one occasion.
 

The female waitresses gave below par service, they were not aware of the menu nor were they at the diner's table to take orders, do follow up, or make sure that their customer was comfortable. They were not aware of the contents of the menu, and had difficulties in communicating with foreign patrons, that were spotted on two reviews.


The male waiter was the epitome of good service, clean cut and attractive in appearance, and very knowledgeable about the items on the menu. One reviewer ate a meal based on his recommendation and was completely satisfied. He was well aware of his customers and makes sure that all their needs were met. His attentions went so far as to be stifling after a point, not allowing the lone diner a quiet moment alone.
 

After having gone through the dilemma of dealing with the waitresses that were incapable of taking correct orders, on one review, the waitress brought two wrong dishes after the reviewer mistakenly decided to place an order in English, oddly enough the language that the menu is in; the bills presented to the reviewers at the end of their meal varied. On two occasions a simple computer print out that did not include the name and location nor VAT registration of the restaurant was handed to them. On another occasion, a reviewer was given a hand written bill that was written on a piece of torn off paper, at least they had folded the lines before they began to cut.
 

The reviewers felt that had the service been up to par, then there would have been more cohesion in the overall feel of the eatery. It was agreed that the billing system was something that had to be amended immediately, particularly for customers that like to keep their receipts.
 
 
Food      

 

The menu offers a wonderful assortment of dishes, starting from appetizers to desserts. Fish, poultry, pork and meat lovers are in for wonderful tantalizers to their pallets, with more of a Chinese cooking method to their dishes. The chef is more than happy to come out and tell you what is in the foods that are being offered, as you are to look in the open kitchen and see what he is cooking up.
 

The sweet and sour Nile Perch was not pleasing to the taste buds of one of the reviewers, being more sweet than sour, and adding a mushy and thick taste to the food. The main dishes were all served with vegetable rice, basically white rice with green beans and carrots in it. On two occasions, the rice was served cold, giving the appearance that it had been cooked in anticipation of the lunch and dinner crowds.
 

The Chicken with Vegetables was a very enjoyable dish; the vegetables were crunchy and not over cooked, while the chicken meat was tender and moist. There was a light sauce over the entire dish, although the reviewer could not decide if the taste was that of oyster sauce or duck sauce, but there was something wonderfully familiar about the tang.
 

The Beef with Oyster Sauce, the recommendation of the male waiter, was excellent with balanced flavours that made the meal quite enjoyable.
 

The appetizers were not as exciting as the main dishes, although their tastes were higher in quality than most other eateries in town that offer the same prices. The Beef Dumplings were a bit oily as was the French Onion Soup. It was a bit of a disappointment to the reviewer, not to find the traditional hardened cheese crust on top.
 

The content of all the dishes offered was large in proportion and able to fill any appetite no matter how large it may be. The freshness of the vegetables and all the ingredients in the food was something that deserves commendation, although the presentation was nothing out of the ordinary.
 
 
 
Price      

 

The prices range from 11-45 Br making this eatery a very affordable one. It is more than possible to have a two-course meal with a drink and still stay under 50 Br. The variety of the dishes that are offered, the quality of the food and the environment make the pricing a great deal for any person that wishes to eat out. This is one of the few new dinning institutions in town that is affordable and attractive to not just the lower and middle level  employee, but to the CEO himself.
 
Environment      

The restaurant is situated on the sixth floor of a recently opened building. The main dining area has an open floor with neat tables set for four or six diners. The walls are a bright canary yellow with scarce and tasteful hangings that accent rather than take from the colouring.
 

The tables and chairs are made of a dark green cast iron and upholstered in green and tan patterns. The white table cloths and the fully set tables along with the choice of colours makes the ambiance suitable for quiet dining either in the evening or during the day.
 

The restaurant also offers a large veranda where there are tables set-up, although a remedy has to be found for the flying tablecloths. The open-air area offers a wonderful view of Addis Abeba and is a great place to have lunch or coffee, the lack of windbreakers though may make the seating uncomfortably cold at night.
 

The most appealing feature about this space is the open view that the diner has into the kitchen and all the activity that is going on in there. For a person that is eating alone, or for a conversation piece, this feature of the restaurant makes the dining experience interesting and full of action.
 
 
Sanitation      

The bathrooms were located behind the cashier, a convenient location that is hidden from view for most of the tables in the restaurant. There are others on the left side of the floor that are forced to face the bathroom head on, although luckily the sight is not one that would kill your appetite.
 

As you walk in, there are two common sinks on one side of the room and two doors on the other leading to the male and female toilets. The bathrooms were clean, with all the amenities to choose from, and most importantly smell free. The space is away from both the kitchen and the main dining areas, which is not something that is common in many restaurants across town.
 

On one review, the many rolled up hand towels that were offered had been used up, and there were no clean ones, perhaps more follow-ups would be an easy remedy to this problem.

 
 
 
Parking          

  

This is the worst feature of the restaurant, with diners having to find a parking space in a 10-car lot that has been built for an entire building. During one review, the one available security guard was in a screaming match with two drivers who were yelling at each other over a parking space.

This is the most immediate issue that needs to be resolved as it is more than likely to repel diners who do not want to wait for 30 minutes just to find a parking spot.
